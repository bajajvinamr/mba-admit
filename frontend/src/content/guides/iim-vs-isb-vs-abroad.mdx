## Three Paths, Very Different Outcomes

If you are an Indian professional considering an MBA, you are almost certainly weighing three options: the IIMs (two-year PGP via CAT), ISB (one-year PGP via GMAT/GRE), or going abroad (usually the US, UK, or Europe). Each path serves a different career strategy, and picking the wrong one costs you years and lakhs.

This is not a ranking exercise. The "best" option depends on where you are now, where you want to be in ten years, and how much risk you are willing to absorb. Here is an honest breakdown.

## The IIM Route: CAT, Two Years, Deep Indian Network

### How It Works
The six older IIMs — [Ahmedabad](/school/iima), Bangalore, Calcutta, Lucknow, Indore, and Kozhikode — admit through the CAT exam, with additional weight on academic record, work experience, and diversity factors. The PGP is a two-year residential program. Newer IIMs (there are now 21 total) follow a similar structure but with different placement outcomes.

### Cost
IIM-A's PGP fee is approximately ₹25 lakh. Including living expenses and opportunity cost, the all-in investment for a working professional earning ₹15-20 LPA is roughly ₹60-70 lakh over two years.

### Placement Reality
The numbers that IIMs publish deserve careful reading. [IIM Ahmedabad](/school/iima) reports an average salary of ₹35 LPA, but this includes a wide distribution. The top quartile earns ₹50+ LPA (consulting, investment banking), while the bottom quartile earns ₹20-25 LPA. Median is more informative than mean here, and medians at the top 3 IIMs sit around ₹30-32 LPA.

IIM-B and IIM-C report similar averages. Below the top 3, there is a noticeable drop — IIM Lucknow and IIM Indore report averages of ₹28-30 LPA, and newer IIMs range from ₹15-22 LPA.

### Strengths
- **Cost efficiency.** The ROI at top IIMs is hard to beat — you break even within 1-2 years.
- **Indian network depth.** IIM alumni run a disproportionate share of Indian corporate leadership. This network compounds over a career.
- **Brand in India.** An IIM-A or IIM-B degree opens every door in the Indian market.
- **Two-year format.** Summer internships allow career exploration, and many students convert their internship into a full-time offer.

### Limitations
- **CAT is a specific skill.** The exam rewards a particular type of test-taking ability that does not always correlate with MBA readiness. Strong professionals sometimes struggle with CAT prep.
- **Limited international placement.** Fewer than 10% of IIM graduates work outside India immediately post-MBA. International recruiters rarely visit IIM campuses.
- **Salary ceiling.** While starting salaries are strong, the long-term earning trajectory in India trails what US or European MBA graduates earn in those markets.
- **Academic rigor varies.** The PGP curriculum at top IIMs is strong, but some newer IIMs have diluted the brand.

## The ISB Route: GMAT, One Year, Accelerated

### How It Works
ISB Hyderabad (with a satellite campus in Mohali) runs a one-year PGP that admits via GMAT, GRE, or ISB's own aptitude test. The program targets professionals with 4+ years of work experience — the average is around 5 years. Class size is approximately 900 across both campuses.

### Cost
Tuition is roughly ₹40 lakh. With living expenses and opportunity cost (one year of lost salary), total investment for someone earning ₹20 LPA is approximately ₹65-70 lakh. Similar to IIMs in total outlay, but compressed into one year.

### Placement Reality
ISB reports an average salary of approximately ₹34 LPA, which has climbed steadily. The distribution is similar to IIMs — consulting and tech roles pull the average up, while operations and general management roles sit lower.

ISB's placement process is more structured than IIMs, with a dedicated career services team and strong recruiter relationships. Amazon, Microsoft, McKinsey, BCG, and Bain are consistent top recruiters.

### Strengths
- **One year.** You lose half the opportunity cost compared to a two-year program. For experienced professionals who do not need an internship to explore careers, this is a major advantage.
- **GMAT-based admission.** If you are already preparing for international schools, ISB uses the same test. You can apply to ISB and US programs simultaneously.
- **International faculty.** ISB's faculty includes visiting professors from Wharton, Kellogg, and other top global schools. The academic exposure has an international flavor.
- **Older, more experienced cohort.** Average age is 28-29, compared to 24-25 at IIMs. Classroom discussions and peer learning benefit from this.

### Limitations
- **No summer internship.** The one-year format means no built-in internship. Career switchers have less room to explore and validate new directions.
- **Single-campus network.** ISB's alumni network is growing fast (20,000+) but is still a fraction of the combined IIM network.
- **Brand perception gap.** In parts of Indian industry, IIM-A/B/C still carry more weight than ISB. This gap is closing but has not disappeared.
- **Compressed timeline.** One year is intense. You have less time to absorb, reflect, and build relationships.

## The Abroad Route: Global Brand, Global Access

### How It Works
"Abroad" usually means M7 and T15 schools in the US ([HBS](/school/hbs), [Stanford GSB](/school/gsb), [Wharton](/school/wharton), [Booth](/school/booth), Kellogg, Columbia, MIT Sloan, etc.), top European programs (INSEAD, LBS, HEC Paris), or UK schools (Oxford Said, Cambridge Judge). Each has a different admission process, program length, and career outcome profile.

For a detailed guide on the US path specifically, see our [MBA in USA from India guide](/guides/mba-in-usa-for-indians).

### Cost
A two-year US MBA costs approximately $200,000-$250,000 all-in (₹1.7-2.1 crore). A one-year European MBA runs $100,000-$150,000 (₹85 lakh-₹1.25 crore). These are full sticker prices — scholarships can reduce them by 20-50%.

### Strengths
- **Global mobility.** An HBS or INSEAD degree works in New York, London, Singapore, Mumbai, or Dubai. IIM and ISB degrees work primarily in India.
- **Salary magnitude.** If you stay in the US or Europe, earnings are 3-5x what you would earn in India with a domestic MBA.
- **Network breadth.** Your classmates will be CEOs, ministers, and founders across 60+ countries. This network is qualitatively different from a domestic one.
- **Career switching.** US programs with two-year formats and robust internship recruiting are the best vehicle for dramatic career pivots.

### Limitations
- **Cost and debt.** ₹2 crore is a serious financial commitment. If you return to India, the payback period stretches to 5-7 years.
- **Visa uncertainty.** H-1B lottery odds for Indians are around 25-30% per year. STEM OPT extensions help, but long-term US work authorization is never guaranteed. See the visa section in our [US MBA guide](/guides/mba-in-usa-for-indians).
- **Opportunity cost.** Two years out of the Indian job market means losing momentum in a fast-moving economy.
- **Admission difficulty.** M7 admit rates for Indian males are in the low single digits. The process is expensive, time-consuming, and uncertain.

## Which Path for Which Goal?

### "I want to stay in India and lead a large company here."
**Go IIM.** The alumni network, brand recognition, and cost efficiency are unmatched for an India-focused career. IIM-A, IIM-B, or IIM-C specifically — the brand premium over other IIMs is significant.

### "I want to work in the US or globally."
**Go abroad.** An IIM degree has limited recognition outside India and South Asia. If global mobility is a priority, you need a globally recognized program. US M7 or INSEAD are the standard choices.

### "I have 5+ years of experience and want to accelerate, not switch."
**ISB is the sweet spot.** One year, strong ROI, experienced cohort. You re-enter the job market quickly at a higher level. If you are already in consulting or tech and want to move up rather than move sideways, ISB's one-year format minimizes disruption.

### "I want to break into consulting or banking from a non-traditional background."
**US two-year program.** The summer internship is critical for career switchers. Consulting firms recruit heavily through the internship pipeline, and converting an offer is easier than lateral hiring. IIMs also work for this path within India, but the internship recruiting infrastructure at US schools is more developed.

### "I want the best ROI regardless of geography."
**IIM or ISB.** The math is clear — at 1/3 to 1/4 the cost, with starting salaries that are strong by Indian standards, domestic programs have the fastest payback. The abroad ROI only wins if you stay abroad long enough for the higher salary to compound.

### "I want to build a startup."
**It depends on where.** For India-focused ventures, IIM-A's CIIE or ISB's DLabs provide good ecosystems. For global startups, Stanford GSB or HBS have the deepest VC networks and the most founder-friendly cultures. INSEAD is strong for startups in Europe, Southeast Asia, or the Middle East.

## The Hybrid Strategy

Many strong applicants hedge by applying to both ISB and top international programs in the same cycle. This works because ISB uses the GMAT (same test), has a similar timeline, and serves as a strong fallback. If you get into both Wharton and ISB, you have a genuine decision to make. If you only get into ISB, you still have an excellent outcome.

The other hybrid: IIM + international. Some professionals do an IIM PGP first, work for 3-5 years, and then pursue a one-year international MBA (INSEAD, LBS) or an executive MBA. This lets you capture the IIM ROI early and add international exposure later when you can better afford it.
